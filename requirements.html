Requirements

1. Introduction

1.1 Purpose of Product

The purpose of our product is to allow users to take notes easily with zero to little limitations to how users want to jot notes. Anyone should be able to record any information with our product and be able to organize their ideas so that it is easy to go back and review any information. Being able to record complex information such as chemical and mathematical formulas should be fast and easy so that students can keep up with a lecture when writing notes.

1.2 Scope of Product

Users should be able to take notes easily. This product is not meant to be a knowledge management platform or a database. This product is designed with students in mind and as such all features are implemented with taking notes during a lecture in mind. For example, students sometimes need to record a table of data in their notes. We will have basic table functionality. However, our product is not a spreadsheet program so any complex functionality such as a sum of cells, spreadsheet automation, math functions created from a selection of cells, etc. are not within the scope of our product.

2.2 Domain Model with Description

Our product's domain is note-taking. Within this domain, the problem we are attempting to solve with our product is that it is oftentimes restricting when taking notes on a computer or mobile device when compared to taking notes with pen and paper. With our product, you will be able to take notes anywhere on the "page", draw anywhere on the "page", make tables, charts and graphs, write complex mathematical formulae, and plot mathematical functions. All of this you will be able to do quickly and easily so you can focus on learning and listening, rather than fiddling with a complex program.

4. System and Non-functional Requirements

4.1 External Interface Requirements (User,Hardware,Software,Communications)


Our note-taking app has the following interfaces:

User Interface: The app will have a graphical user interface (GUI) that allows users to create, edit, and organize their notes. The interface should be intuitive and user-friendly.
- NF.4.1.1: The app should have a simple and clean design with a minimalist layout.
- NF.4.1.2: The app should have a search functionality to help users find notes quickly.
- NF.4.1.3: The app should allow users to organize their notes into categories or folders.

Hardware Interface: The app will require minimal hardware resources to run on a user's device, such as memory and processing power.
- NF.4.1.4: The app should be compatible with various device types, including smartphones, tablets, and desktop computers.

Software Interface: The app will require access to the device's operating system and other software components to function correctly.
- NF.4.1.5: The app should be compatible with various operating systems, including Android, iOS, and Windows.

Communications Interface: The app will require internet connectivity to sync notes across devices and enable collaboration features.
- NF.4.1.6: The app should support cloud syncing with popular services like Google Drive and Dropbox.
- NF.4.1.7: The app should have end-to-end encryption to protect users' data during transmission.

4.2 Performance Requirements


Our note-taking app needs to provide a seamless user experience with fast and responsive performance.

- NF.4.2.1: The app should load quickly and be responsive to user actions.
- NF.4.2.2: The app should have minimal lag or delay when typing or editing notes.
- NF.4.2.3: The app should be able to handle a large number of notes without slowing down.
- NF.4.2.4: The app should have a backup system that saves notes automatically to prevent data loss.

4.3 Design Constraints


Our note-taking app will need to conform to specific design constraints to provide a consistent user experience.

- NF.4.3.1: The app should have a simple and uncluttered design that emphasizes functionality over aesthetics.
- NF.4.3.2: The app should adhere to standard design patterns and conventions to make it easy for users to learn and use.
- NF.4.3.3: The app should be accessible to users with disabilities, including support for screen readers and other assistive technologies.

4.4 Quality Requirements


Our users expect high-quality performance and reliable functionality from our note-taking app.
While it is not a life-critical system, data loss or corruption can be frustrating and inconvenient.

- NF.4.4.1: The app should be thoroughly tested to ensure reliability and prevent crashes or errors.
- NF.4.4.2: The app should have regular updates and bug fixes to maintain functionality and security.
- NF.4.4.3: The app should have a backup and restore feature to prevent data loss or corruption.

4.5 Other Requirements


- NF.4.5.1: The app should have a password or biometric authentication system to prevent unauthorized access to notes.
- NF.4.5.2: The app should have an option to export notes to various file formats, such as PDF or plain text.
- NF.4.5.3: The app should have a feature that allows users to share notes with others, either by email or through collaboration platforms like Google Docs.
